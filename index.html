What I do

User Research

I dig deep to learn about user needs, workflows, and expectations. I talk directly to users and system stakeholders in the system, and observe current-state workflows to get the full picture.

Tools/Methods used: Interviews, focus groups, field studies, surveys

Analysis

I turn all my user research into workflow assessments and stakeholder needs. I analyze quantitative data to find patterns and points for additional investigation. I list the user’s tasks and potential use errors, then trace them to design requirements to mitigate safety risks. If it’s a medical device, I make sure it complies with the IEC 62366 standard and FDA guidance on usability engineering.

Tools/Methods used: Task analysis, usability risk assessment, statistics (R), VBA for Excel

Design

I start with low-fidelity wireframes to illustrate concepts. As I iterate on those designs with end users and the development team, I move on to screen design and interactive prototypes. I document the UX design in design specifications. I make the occasional website too — I made this website template from scratch with HTML, CSS, and the Hugo static site generator.

Tools/Methods used: Sketch, Axure, InVision, Balsamiq, PHP, JavaScript/jQuery

Evaluation

Throughout design and development, I collect user feedback and observe user performance with the UX design as often as I can. Before launch (or regulatory submission), I test the system with users to make sure it’s safe and intuitive for use. For smaller projects with no safety impact, I have facilitated expert review.

Tools/Methods used: Usability testing, heuristic evaluation, design verification
